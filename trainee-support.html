Support for Trainees

Resources and guidance for your wellbeing and professional development

Training can be challenging, and it's important to know that support is available. This page outlines the various resources and services that can help you throughout your GP training journey. If you're experiencing difficulties, please reach out through the formal channels available to you.

This page provides general information about support services. For specific concerns, difficulties, or formal processes, you should contact your Educational Supervisor, Training Programme Director, Director of Medical Education, or your local postgraduate medical education team. All formal processes, including those related to performance concerns, ARCP outcomes, or trainees in difficulty, should be managed through the appropriate official channels as outlined in the Gold Guide and local policies. This information is provided for guidance only and does not constitute formal advice.

Professional Support Unit (PSU)

The Professional Support Unit (PSU) is a free service dedicated to supporting doctors and dentists in postgraduate training. The PSU operates independently of specialty schools, training programmes, or Trusts and offers confidential and impartial support.

Who Can Access PSU?

Trainees can access the PSU for support on a range of topics including:

  • Difficulty passing examinations
  • Relationship difficulties (with patients or colleagues)
  • Career doubts or uncertainty
  • Low motivation or confidence
  • Personal factors which impact on work
  • Concerns about possible dyslexia or other learning differences

Services Available

One-to-One Support

  • Coaching and career coaching
  • Linguistic and communication skills support
  • Signposting to other agencies

E-Learning Resources

  • Time management
  • Exam preparation
  • Stress management
  • Mental health and wellbeing

Workshops & Groups

  • Exam preparation workshops
  • Assertiveness training
  • Interview preparation
  • Peer support groups
  • Burnout awareness workshops
üí° How to Access

Access to the PSU is by self-referral. Contact your local postgraduate medical education team or visit your deanery/HEE website for specific contact details and application forms. If you're unsure which services would be most useful, you can complete a general application form or speak to a PSU Coordinator.

Single Point of Contact (SPOC) Support

SPOC is a free, confidential, and impartial service offering 45-minute appointments with senior advisors within the Professional Support Unit. These appointments provide a neutral, safe space to discuss any difficulties or dilemmas you're facing.

SPOC is Suitable If You:

  • Want a neutral, safe, and private space to talk freely about a difficult experience
  • Are feeling worried, overwhelmed, stressed, or finding it hard to cope at work
  • Want guidance about what support may be most helpful
  • Need signposting to other resources
  • Want help finding a useful way forward in your current situation

SPOC appointments can be one-off conversations or, if necessary, can include follow-up appointments. Contact your local PSU or postgraduate medical education team to request a confidential one-to-one appointment.

Supported Return to Training (SuppoRTT)

The Supported Return to Training (SuppoRTT) programme aims to support all doctors and dentists to safely and confidently return to training after a sustained period of absence.

SuppoRTT can help with:

  • Planning your return to training
  • Confidence building after a period away
  • Clinical skills updates
  • Reintegration support
  • Financial support for return to training activities
üìã Eligibility

SuppoRTT is available to trainees returning after any absence of more than three months, including parental leave, sick leave, or other approved leave. Contact your Training Programme Director or local SuppoRTT coordinator for more information and to apply.

Additional Support Services

Employee Assistance Programmes (EAP)

Many organisations provide free, confidential counselling services through Employee Assistance Programmes (EAP) or Staff Health services. These are usually accessible through:

  • Your Occupational Health website
  • Your local Trust intranet
  • Your HR Department

BMA Wellbeing Support Services

The BMA offers a free telephone counselling service available 24/7 for any doctor, not just BMA members. This service provides immediate support when you need it most.

Other Resources

  • Academy of Medical Royal Colleges: Provides a range of organisations, services, and websites offering help and support
  • Samaritans: Available 24/7 for anyone in distress (call 116 123)
  • Practitioner Health: Specialist service for doctors and dentists with mental health or addiction concerns

Free Access to Wellbeing Apps

NHS colleagues have free access to various wellbeing apps, including:

  • Unmind: Mental health platform with tools for stress, sleep, and resilience
  • Headspace: Meditation and mindfulness app
  • Other approved wellbeing applications

The full list of available apps can be accessed through the NHS England website or your local Trust intranet. Check with your Occupational Health or HR department for access codes.

Less Than Full Time (LTFT) Training

NHS England is committed to increasing flexibility in postgraduate medical training and fully supports Less Than Full Time (LTFT) Training.

‚úÖ Eligibility

All doctors in training can now apply to train LTFT for any well-founded reason, including:

  • Wellbeing reasons
  • Personal choice
  • Carers responsibilities
  • Health reasons
  • Other well-founded reasons

Application Process

Applications for LTFT training should be submitted through the appropriate channels:

  • Wellbeing, carers, or health: Can apply at any point during your rotation
  • Other criteria: Applications should be made during LTFT application windows aligned to your rotation month
  • Timing: All applications should be submitted 16 weeks ahead of rotation to allow Trusts time to adjust rotas

Further details on applying to work LTFT and FAQs can be found through your local postgraduate medical education support portal. Contact your Training Programme Director for guidance.

Financial Support

Relocation and Excess Travel Expenses

Postgraduate doctors and dentists in training can claim up to ¬£10,000 to cover relocation and excess mileage costs over the duration of their postgraduate training. This can be used for:

  • Finding and moving to new accommodation if relocating as part of training
  • Additional travel costs if your training programme covers a large geographical area

Important: Check your eligibility for travel expenses before submitting your claim. Relocation expenses are processed by your employing Trust. Contact the Medical Staffing team at your Trust for further advice on how to apply.

Early Reimbursement of Study Leave Expenses

The process of early reimbursement for study leave expenses allows trainees to apply for reimbursement as soon as a booking is made, rather than waiting until after a course has been attended.

  • Reimbursement is made by your employing Trust based on actual spend against receipts
  • Funds will reach you within the next possible payroll payment
  • Applies to prebooked course fees, travel, and accommodation expenses
  • Must be approved by your Educational Supervisor (essential/supporting courses) or TPD and Head of School (discretionary courses)

Contact your Trust PGME team if you have any queries regarding the early reimbursement process.

NHS England Sexual Misconduct Policy

NHS England is committed to taking a zero-tolerance approach to sexual misconduct in the workplace to create a culture where everybody feels safe.

The policy:

  • Outlines the approach to tackling sexual misconduct and dealing with perpetrators
  • Describes what sexual misconduct is and how to report it
  • Adopts and signposts to processes in other NHS England employment policies
  • Provides a summary of support available to employees

Support is available throughout the process.

Support for Trainees in Difficulty

If you're experiencing difficulties in training, it's important to know that early identification and intervention can help resolve concerns. The system is designed to support you while maintaining patient safety.

What is a Trainee in Difficulty?

A trainee in difficulty is one whose progress is causing concern or who is not meeting curricular requirements. This may be due to:

  • Ill health
  • Life events
  • Difficulties with learning
  • Less than satisfactory professional conduct

Early Warning Signs

Research has identified key early warning signs that may indicate a trainee is in difficulty:

1. The Disappearing Act

Lateness, unexplained absence, unreliability

2. Slow Work Rate

Failing to engage with e-Portfolio, few log entries, incomplete WPBA

3. Ward Rage

Outbursts of temper

4. Rigidity

Poor tolerance of ambiguity, inability to compromise

5. Bypass Syndrome

Nurses and others avoid seeking opinions from trainee

6. Career Problems

Difficulty with exams, uncertainty about career choice

7. Lack of Self-Awareness

Rejection of constructive criticism, defensiveness

How Concerns Are Managed

The management of concerns follows a structured approach:

Low Level Concerns

  • Discussed with the trainee
  • Documented in the e-portfolio
  • Reported to Educational Supervisor and Training Programme Director
  • With early intervention, should be resolved without further escalation

Even if resolved, concerns should be documented and communicated through the Educational Supervisor's Report for consideration at ARCP.

Intermediate or High Level Concerns

  • Discussed with Associate Postgraduate Dean
  • Meeting arranged with trainee, Clinical Supervisor, Educational Supervisor, Training Programme Director, and/or Associate Postgraduate Dean
  • Action plan developed with clearly defined measurable outcomes
  • Regular review meetings scheduled
  • Consideration of additional support (Occupational Health, NCAS, etc.)

All concerns must be documented and brought to the attention of the ARCP panel.

ARCP Outcomes

If an unsatisfactory ARCP outcome is thought to be possible:

  • The trainee must be informed in advance
  • All documentation must be shared with the trainee prior to the ARCP panel hearing
  • An unsatisfactory outcome may lead to:
    • A period of targeted training
    • A requirement to undergo a defined further period of training
    • Additional support (careers support, LTFT training, occupational health referral)

It should never come as a surprise to trainees that formal action is under consideration. Any shortcomings should be identified and discussed as soon as they become apparent. All decisions should be recorded in writing. If you have concerns about your progress, speak to your Educational Supervisor or Training Programme Director at the earliest opportunity.

Key People Who Can Help

If you need support, these are the key people you can contact:

Educational Supervisor (ES)

Your primary educational support throughout training. They can help with portfolio, learning needs, and career guidance.

Training Programme Director (TPD)

Manages the training programme and can help with programme-related concerns, rotations, and progression issues.

Clinical Supervisor (CS)

Your day-to-day supervisor in your current placement. They can help with immediate clinical and workplace concerns.

Director of Medical Education (DME)

Trust-level support for educational and training matters. Can help with Trust-specific issues and resources.

Remember: You're Not Alone

Training can be challenging, and it's completely normal to need support at various points in your journey. The most important thing is to reach out early if you're struggling. Whether it's exam stress, workplace difficulties, personal challenges, or concerns about your progress, there are people and services ready to help you. Don't hesitate to contact your Educational Supervisor, Training Programme Director, or any of the support services outlined above.
