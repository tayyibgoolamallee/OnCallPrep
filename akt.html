Applied Knowledge Test - Complete Study Guide

What is the AKT?

The Applied Knowledge Test (AKT) is a computer-based exam that tests your knowledge of evidence-based medicine, clinical guidelines, and primary care practice. It's one of the two exams required for MRCGP.

Updated Format (October 2025)

  • Total questions: 160 (reduced from 200)
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Question types: Single Best Answer (SBA) questions
  • Content: Clinical Medicine, Evidence-Based Practice (EBP), and Patient-Centred Care (PCOM)
  • Pass mark: Approximately 70% (varies by sitting)

Exam Structure

Question Distribution

  • Clinical Medicine: ~80% of questions (128 questions)
  • EBP (Evidence-Based Practice): ~10% of questions (16 questions)
  • PCOM (Patient-Centred Care): ~10% of questions (16 questions)

3-Month Study Schedule

Month 1: Foundation

  • Review major clinical topics systematically
  • Focus on NICE guidelines and CKS
  • Start question bank practice (untimed)
  • Build knowledge base

Month 2: Consolidation

  • Continue question bank practice
  • Focus on weak areas identified
  • Practice timed questions
  • Review EBP and PCOM topics

Month 3: Exam Conditions

  • Full mock exams under timed conditions
  • Final review of guidelines
  • Focus on high-yield topics
  • Mental preparation and exam technique

Three Study Strategy Modes

1. Untimed Mode

Learn at your own pace. Read explanations carefully, look up guidelines, understand the reasoning. Best for early study.

2. Timed with Notes

Practice under time pressure but allow yourself to make notes. Builds speed while maintaining learning. Best for mid-study.

3. Exam Conditions

Full exam simulation - timed, no notes, no looking up answers. Best for final preparation and identifying weak areas.

IMG Tips - Special Considerations

If you're an International Medical Graduate, here are 13 key strategies to help you succeed:

1. Understand UK Guidelines

UK practice differs significantly from many countries. Focus on NICE guidelines, CKS, and BNF. These are the gold standard for AKT.

2. Evidence-Based Practice

EBP questions test statistics and study design. If this is new, dedicate extra time. Practice calculating NNT, NNH, ARR, RRR.

3. Patient-Centred Care

PCOM questions test communication, shared decision-making, and UK healthcare values. Understand concepts like patient autonomy, capacity, and consent.

4. Common Pitfalls

  • Over-investigation: UK GPs investigate less than many countries
  • Prescribing: Know BNF well - UK has different drugs and protocols
  • Referral thresholds: UK GPs manage more in primary care
  • Terminology: Learn UK medical terms and abbreviations

5. Study Resources

  • NICE guidelines (free online)
  • CKS (Clinical Knowledge Summaries)
  • BNF (British National Formulary)
  • RCGP AKT question banks
  • GP training materials

6. Language & Communication

If English isn't your first language, practice reading medical English quickly. AKT is time-pressured. Consider medical English courses if needed.

7. Cultural Context

Understand UK healthcare culture - patient expectations, NHS structure, primary care role. This helps with PCOM questions.

8. Time Management

160 questions in 3 hours = 1.125 minutes per question. Practice speed reading and quick decision-making.

9. Question Bank Priority

Use UK-specific question banks. International question banks may teach different approaches that won't work for AKT.

10. Study Groups

Join IMG study groups. Share experiences, discuss questions, learn from others facing similar challenges.

11. Mock Exams

Take multiple full mock exams. Get used to the format, timing, and question style. Identify patterns in your mistakes.

12. Weak Areas

Be honest about weak areas. EBP statistics, UK guidelines, or PCOM concepts? Focus extra time there.

13. Exam Day

Arrive early, bring ID, stay calm. Read questions carefully. Don't spend too long on one question - mark for review and move on.
