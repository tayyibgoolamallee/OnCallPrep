Reflection Writing Guide

How to write effective portfolio reflections

Effective reflections demonstrate learning, self-awareness, and professional development. This guide will help you write reflections that meet portfolio requirements and show genuine growth.

The Three-Link Rule

Key Principle

Every reflection should link to at least three competencies. This demonstrates holistic thinking and integration of multiple skills.

Why Three Links?

  • Shows holistic understanding of patient care
  • Demonstrates integration of multiple skills
  • Reflects real-world complexity
  • Shows reflective practice at a higher level

Example Links

Good Example

Case: Managing a patient with chest pain

Links:

  • Clinical Knowledge: Differential diagnosis of chest pain
  • Communication: Explaining risk and management options
  • Patient Safety: Appropriate investigation and safety netting

Reflection Structure

1
What happened?

Brief description of the case or event. Keep it concise - focus on what's relevant.

2
What did you think/feel?

Your initial thoughts, concerns, or feelings. This shows self-awareness.

3
What did you learn?

The key learning points. What will you do differently next time?

4
How will this change your practice?

Specific actions you'll take. Show application of learning.
