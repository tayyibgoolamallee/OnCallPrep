ST3 WPBA Requirements

Based on RCGP WPBA Requirements. Minimum requirements per 6-month placement:

  • ‚úì Case-Based Discussions (CBDs): Minimum 6 per 6-month placement

    Complex cases, independent decision-making, demonstrate readiness for independent practice.

  • ‚úì Consultation Observation Tools (COTs): Minimum 4 per GP placement

    Should demonstrate safe, efficient, patient-centred consulting at independent practice level.

  • ‚úì Multi-Source Feedback (MSF): 1 per year + 1 Leadership MSF

    Minimum 8 responses. Leadership MSF should reflect developing leadership skills.

  • ‚úì Patient Satisfaction Questionnaire (PSQ): 1 per GP placement (Required in ST3)

    Minimum 20 patient responses required. This is only required in ST3, not ST1 or ST2.

  • ‚úì Reflective Entries: Minimum 6 per 6-month placement

    Part of 36 reflections per year total. Should link to 3+ competencies, demonstrate learning.

  • ‚úì Learning Log: Regular entries, including SCA preparation

    Document all learning: clinical cases, courses, reading, SCA preparation.

  • ‚úì Personal Development Plan (PDP): Focus on areas for ongoing development post-CCT

    Should be updated regularly, show progression, identify learning needs for post-CCT practice.

  • ‚úì Clinical Supervisor Report (CSR): From each placement

    Required from every clinical supervisor at end of placement.

üìù Remember: You need 36 reflections per year total, typically split over 2-3 rotations (4-6 months each). This means approximately 12-18 reflections per rotation depending on your training structure.
üéØ SCA Preparation (ST3 Focus)

When to take SCA: Usually taken in ST3, after passing AKT. Plan for 2-3 months of dedicated practice.

What is the SCA?

The Simulated Consultation Assessment tests your ability to manage consultations in a realistic setting. You'll have 12 minutes per case to demonstrate clinical reasoning, communication, and management skills.

Study Strategy

  • Practice cases: Work through mock cases regularly
  • Time management: Practice explaining diagnoses in 2 minutes
  • Priming exercises: Practice 3-minute priming scenarios
  • Self-assessment: Use structured checklists to assess your performance
  • Video practice: Record yourself if possible

Key Domains

  • Domain 1: Data gathering and interpretation
  • Domain 2: Clinical management (weighted 1.5x)
  • Domain 3: Interpersonal skills

GPST3: Month-by-Month (Transition to Independent GP)

This is approximate, because rotations differ ‚Äî but the emotional and developmental pattern is remarkably consistent.

Early GPST3: "Everyone thinks I should know this"

Responsibility suddenly feels real. You may feel lonely with decisions. This is normal.

Good GPST3 trainees:
  • Discuss their reasoning, not just outcomes
  • Admit uncertainty early
  • Learn how local services actually work

Late GPST3: Letting go

Confidence and anxiety often sit side by side here. You're not aiming to feel fearless ‚Äî just appropriately cautious and reflective.

If you're still struggling at this stage, escalation is not a disaster. Many strong GPs needed extra time or tailored support.

Strategic Focus Areas

1. Readiness for Independent Practice

Handle complex multi-morbidity confidently. Manage uncertainty appropriately. Demonstrate safe practice. Show awareness of limitations and when to seek help.

2. Leadership & Development

Teaching and supervision activities. Quality improvement projects. Practice management understanding. Career planning and next steps.

3. Exam Completion

Ensure AKT is passed (if not done in ST2). Plan SCA date early in ST3. Balance exam prep with portfolio work. Prepare for ARCP and CCT application.

Quality Improvement & Audits

QI Projects in ST3

QI projects demonstrate leadership and commitment to improving patient care. Essential for portfolio and career development.

QI Methodology: PDSA Cycle

1
Plan

Identify problem, set SMART goals, plan interventions, decide what data to collect

2
Do

Implement changes on a small scale, collect data

3
Study

Analyze results, what worked? What didn't? What did you learn?

4
Act

Embed changes, scale up, or try again with modifications

QI Project Ideas for ST3

  • Improving medication review processes
  • Enhancing patient access to services
  • Improving chronic disease management
  • Reducing DNAs (Did Not Attend)
  • Improving vaccination uptake
  • Enhancing patient safety measures

Prescribing Assessment

As part of your training, you'll need to complete a prescribing assessment. This demonstrates your safe prescribing practices and understanding of medication management.

  • Review your prescribing patterns
  • Identify areas for improvement
  • Complete any required assessments
  • Document learning points

Leadership & Feedback: MSF and PSQ

üìä Feedback Requirements

MSF (Multi-Source Feedback) and PSQ (Patient Satisfaction Questionnaire) are essential components of your portfolio, demonstrating your ability to work effectively with colleagues and patients.

MSF/PSQ Requirements for ST3

  • MSF: Minimum 1 per year, aim for 8+ responses
  • PSQ: 1 per GP placement, minimum 20 patient responses
  • Complete these early in placements to allow time for reflection and action planning
  • Use feedback constructively to identify areas for development

Leadership MSF

In ST3, your MSF should reflect your developing leadership skills. Consider asking:

  • Clinical supervisors
  • Practice managers
  • Practice nurses
  • Administrative staff
  • Peers (if appropriate)

The SHARP Model for Feedback

Processing Feedback Effectively

  • Share - Acknowledge the feedback
  • Hear - Listen actively, don't become defensive
  • Acknowledge - Thank the person giving feedback
  • Reflect - Think about what you've heard
  • Plan - Decide what you'll do differently

Responding to Feedback

  • Thank those who provided feedback
  • Reflect on both positive and constructive feedback
  • Create an action plan for areas identified for development
  • Document your reflection in your portfolio
  • Share your action plan with your supervisor
  • Follow up on your progress

Feedback is a gift - it helps you grow. Even if feedback is difficult to hear, use it constructively. Your response to feedback demonstrates professionalism and readiness for independent practice.

Career Planning (ST3)

ST3 is also time to think about your career post-CCT:

  • Practice type: Partnership, salaried, locum, portfolio career?
  • Special interests: What areas do you want to develop?
  • Location: Where do you want to work?
  • Further training: Any additional qualifications or courses?

One Last Thing I Want You to Hear

Needing help during GP training does not predict being a poor GP.

It often predicts being a careful, reflective, humane one.

First, something important: Most GP trainees who struggle are not unsafe, lazy, or unsuitable. They are conscientious, tired, and holding too much in their heads.

If at any point while reading this you think "this sounds like me", that's not a bad sign ‚Äî it's usually the opposite.
