VTS and ST Structure

Familiarize yourself with the GP training structure to better support your trainees through their journey.

GP Training Structure Overview

Three-Year Training Program

ST1 - Foundation Year

  • Focus: Building foundation skills and confidence
  • Placements: Hospital rotations + first GP placement
  • Key assessments: CBDs, reflections, MSF
  • Emphasis: Learning the system, getting comfortable with GP

ST2 - Development Year

  • Focus: Progression and development
  • Placements: Hospital rotations + GP placements
  • Key assessments: Increased CBDs, COTs, PSQ
  • Emphasis: Demonstrating development, AKT preparation

ST3 - Exit Year

  • Focus: Readiness for independent practice
  • Placements: Predominantly GP, some hospital (if needed)
  • Key assessments: Complex cases, leadership, SCA preparation
  • Emphasis: Demonstrating readiness, career planning

ARCP (Annual Review of Competence Progression)

Understanding the ARCP process helps you support trainees effectively:

  • Occurs every 12 months (or more frequently if concerns)
  • Reviews trainee progress against curriculum
  • Outcomes: Satisfactory progress, requirements for improvement, or concerns
  • Your role: Ensure trainees are well-prepared and supported

Planning Residentials & Socials

üéâ Building Community

Residentials and social events are important for trainee wellbeing, networking, and building a supportive training community.

Residential Events

Planning Considerations

Logistics

  • Venue selection (accessibility, accommodation, facilities)
  • Catering arrangements
  • Travel and transport
  • Budget planning and funding
  • Accessibility requirements

Program Content

  • Educational sessions (portfolio, exams, clinical topics)
  • Workshops and group activities
  • Networking opportunities
  • Social activities
  • Balance of education and social time

Social Events

  • Regular meetups (coffee, meals, informal gatherings)
  • End-of-rotation celebrations
  • Year-group social events
  • Networking opportunities with other cohorts
  • Wellbeing-focused activities

Top Tips for Planning

  • Start planning early - venues and dates book up quickly
  • Involve trainees in planning - get their input on what they want
  • Consider diverse needs - dietary requirements, accessibility, costs
  • Balance educational and social time
  • Create opportunities for cross-year interaction
  • Budget carefully - consider trainee costs and available funding
  • Have a backup plan for weather-dependent activities
