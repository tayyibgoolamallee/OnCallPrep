ST2 Guide

AKT Preparation & Increased Complexity

ST2 is your development year. You should demonstrate increasing complexity in your cases, more independent decision-making, and begin serious AKT preparation. Show clear progression from ST1.

üéØ AKT Preparation (ST2 Focus)

When to take AKT: Most trainees take AKT in ST2 or early ST3. Plan for 3 months of dedicated study.

Study Strategy

  • Start early: Begin 3-4 months before your planned exam date
  • Use question banks: Practice with AKT-style questions regularly
  • Focus on guidelines: NICE, CKS, BNF are essential
  • Clinical topics: Cover all major systems systematically
  • Mock exams: Practice under timed conditions

Key Topics to Cover

  • Cardiovascular (hypertension, heart failure, AF)
  • Respiratory (asthma, COPD, infections)
  • Diabetes and endocrine
  • Mental health (depression, anxiety, dementia)
  • Women's health (contraception, menopause)
  • Child health (common presentations)
  • EBP & PCOM (evidence-based practice, patient-centred care)

Strategic Focus Areas

1. Increased Complexity

  • Choose cases with multiple differentials
  • Demonstrate independent decision-making
  • Show management of uncertainty
  • Handle more complex multi-morbidity

2. Portfolio Progression

  • Show clear improvement from ST1
  • Demonstrate deeper reflection
  • Link to more advanced competencies
  • Show leadership and teaching activities

3. Exam Preparation

  • Balance portfolio work with AKT study
  • Document AKT study in learning log
  • Use clinical cases to reinforce learning
  • Plan exam date around placements
