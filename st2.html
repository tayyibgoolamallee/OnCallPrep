ST2 WPBA Requirements

Based on RCGP WPBA Requirements. Minimum requirements per 6-month placement:

  • ‚úì Case-Based Discussions (CBDs): Minimum 6 per 6-month placement

    Should demonstrate increasing complexity and independent decision-making.

  • ‚úì Consultation Observation Tools (COTs): Minimum 4 per GP placement

    Should show progression in consultation skills.

  • ‚úì Multi-Source Feedback (MSF): 1 per year

    Minimum 8 responses from colleagues across different roles.

  • ‚úì Reflective Entries: Minimum 6 per 6-month placement

    Part of 36 reflections per year total. Should link to 3+ competencies, demonstrate learning.

  • ‚úì Learning Log: Regular entries, including AKT study activities

    Document all learning: clinical cases, courses, reading, AKT study.

  • ‚úì Personal Development Plan (PDP): Updated and reviewed with ES

    Should be updated regularly, show progression, identify learning needs.

  • ‚úì Clinical Supervisor Report (CSR): From each placement

    Required from every clinical supervisor at end of placement.

üìù Remember: You need 36 reflections per year total, typically split over 2-3 rotations (4-6 months each). This means approximately 12-18 reflections per rotation depending on your training structure.
üéØ AKT Preparation (ST2 Focus)

When to take AKT: Most trainees take AKT in ST2 or early ST3. Plan for 3 months of dedicated study.

Study Strategy

  • Start early: Begin 3-4 months before your planned exam date
  • Use question banks: Practice with AKT-style questions regularly
  • Focus on guidelines: NICE, CKS, BNF are essential
  • Clinical topics: Cover all major systems systematically
  • Mock exams: Practice under timed conditions

Key Topics to Cover

  • Cardiovascular (hypertension, heart failure, AF)
  • Respiratory (asthma, COPD, infections)
  • Diabetes and endocrine
  • Mental health (depression, anxiety, dementia)
  • Women's health (contraception, menopause)
  • Child health (common presentations)
  • EBP & PCOM (evidence-based practice, patient-centred care)

GPST2: Month-by-Month (Integration & Pressure)

This is approximate, because rotations differ ‚Äî but the emotional and developmental pattern is remarkably consistent.

Early GPST2: Slower, and worried about it

If you start GP placements here, you may suddenly feel incompetent again. Ten minutes feels impossible. Your brain feels full.

This does not mean you're bad at GP. It means you're learning a new skill set.

What helps

Use a clear consultation structure every time. Say out loud to your supervisor:

"I'm safe, but slow ‚Äî can we work on that together?"

That is a very reasonable request.

Mid GPST2: The quiet dip

This is where motivation often drops. You might notice: irritability, doubting your ability, avoiding portfolio or feedback.

This is a warning phase, not a failure phase.

Do not push through silently. This is when to speak to your ES and, if needed, your TPD ‚Äî not because you're in trouble, but because support works best here.

Strategic Focus Areas

1. Increased Complexity

Choose cases with multiple differentials. Demonstrate independent decision-making. Show management of uncertainty. Handle more complex multi-morbidity.

2. Portfolio Progression

Show clear improvement from ST1. Demonstrate deeper reflection. Link to more advanced competencies. Show leadership and teaching activities.

3. Exam Preparation

Balance portfolio work with AKT study. Document AKT study in learning log. Use clinical cases to reinforce learning. Plan exam date around placements.
